Chāyā Pūjā

Grahaṇa Rahasya Sadhana

You are now ready for Grahaṇa Rahasya Sādhana. Follow the steps below with faith, not fear.

This practice is simple yet immensely powerful, invoking the combined energies of Śrī Kālabhairava and Śrī Mā Kāmakhyā.

Kālabhairava — Master of time, remover of obstacles, fearless guardian.
Mā Kāmakhyā — Primordial Shakti, fulfiller of desires, awakener of attraction and inner power.

Together, they grant protection, strength, and fulfillment, clearing your path and awakening your deepest potential.

True Grahaṇa Sādhana blossoms when the mind is free of lust, greed, or anticipation—rooted in devotion and complete surrender.

Preparation

  • 1 Fasting (Upavāsa): Begin fasting from the previous day afternoon or at least 6 hours before the eclipse. Only water or light fruits allowed.
  • 2 Śuddhi (Purification): Take a bath before and after gharana (Add 1 spoon of turmeric powder to water and take Headbathe). Wear clean clothes (preferably red for Devi or black for Bhairava).
  • 3 Āsana: Prepare a kusha, woolen, or black/red cloth seat facing South only.
  • 4 Altar: Place images/idols of Kala Bhairava and Kāmakhyā Mā on a clean wooden platform or as comfortably as you can arrange. Light a lamp with ghee or sesame oil.
  • 5 Offerings: Flowers (red/white), incense, akṣata, fruits (Pañcamakāra Puja Optional - see below).
  • 6 Sankalpa: Mentally resolve your sādhana intention – protection, siddhi, inner awakening, or fulfillment of a wish.

Pañcamakāram Pujā

This is the heart of Vāmācāra Sadhana and offer with love and surrender not with fear and lust. If not initiated, use symbolic substitutes.

  • 1 Madya (Wine) – Drink wine (After Gharana) / substitute with honey or milk.
  • 2 Mamsa (Meat) – Offer meat / substitute with fruits or dry nuts.
  • 3 Matsya (Fish) – Offer fish / substitute with black sesame seeds.
  • 4 Mudrā (Gesture / Grain) – Use hand gestures or offer parched grain.
  • 5 Maithuna (Union) – Symbolic internal union of Shiva & Shakti, Visualize the Shiva Lingam seated upon the Yoni Peeta, radiant with divine energy, uniting Shakti and Shiva in perfect harmony.

Arrange the four sacred offerings before the idols of Bhairava and Mā Kāmakhyā, and illuminate a lamp, invoking their blessings with faith and reverence.

Chant During Offering:

"ॐ ह्रीं कामाख्यायै भैरव स्वाहा॥"
"Om Hrīṁ Kāmākhyāyai Bhairavaya Svāhā॥"

Om Asya Shri Kamakhya Bhairava Maha-Mantrasya... Brahma Rishi, Gayatri Chhandah, Kamakhya Bhaiarava Devta, Mam Sarva Manokamana Siddhyarthe Jape Viniyoga

Nyāsa & Mudrā

Perform kara-nyāsa and anga-nyāsa with Bhairava/Kāmakhyā bīja mantras.

  • ●Place right hand on heart – chant "Klim-Bum, Hṛdayāya namaḥ"
  • ●On head – "Klim-Bum, Śirase svāhā"
  • ●On shoulders – "Klim-Bum, Śikhāyai vauṣaṭ"
  • ●On both eyes – "Klim-Bum, Kavacāya hum"
  • ●Across body – "Klim-Bum, Astrāya phaṭ"

Japa Mantra

(11 Mala Each - 1188 japa or as per capacity)

ॐ ह्रां ह्रीं ह्रौं कालय भैरवाय नमः॥
Om Hrām Hrīm Hraum Bum Kālabhairavāya Namaḥ॥
ॐ क्लीं कामाख्यायै नमः॥
Om Klīṁ Kāmākhyāyai Namaḥ॥

After Mantra japa, recite "Kleem Kleem Kaamaakhyaa Kleem Kleem namah |" for 3-5 Malas.

At Mokṣa Kāla (End of Eclipse)

  • ● Take a bath immediately after eclipse ends and wear clean clothes again.
  • ● Perform pūrṇāhuti - Mantra:
    ॐ पूर्णमदः पूर्णमिदं पूर्णात् पूर्णमुदच्यते । पूर्णस्य पूर्णमादाय पूर्णमेवावशिष्यते ॥ ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥
  • ● Offer Arghya to Chandra/Surya with water, rice, flowers – praying for calm mind, clarity, blessings (If unable to go out due to climate condition, then close your eyes and visualize the chandra and perform the steps).
  • ● Do Kṣamā Prārthanā (asking forgiveness for mistakes in mantra/ritual). Mantra:

    ॐ सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः (Om Sarve Bhavantu Sukhinah)
    सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः (Sarve Santu Niraamayaah) ।
    सर्वे भद्राणि पश्यन्तु (Sarve Bhadrani Pashyantu)
    मा कश्चिद्दुःखभाग्भवेत् (Maa Kashchit Dukha Bhaag Bhavet) ॥
    ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥ (Om Shanti Shanti Shantih)
Disclaimer: This sādhana is meant only for performance during the Eclipse (Grahaṇa Kāla).
It should not be undertaken as a Mandala practice or as any other form of regular sādhana.
